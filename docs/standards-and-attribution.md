# Standards and attribution

OWASP is the organizing backbone for this skill because its projects span application, API, AI, agentic, mobile, cloud-native, Kubernetes, CI/CD, and supply-chain security. The skill distinguishes verification standards from awareness and prioritization lists.

## Usage policy

- ASVS is used as a verification baseline for applicable application controls.
- Top 10 lists are used for prioritization and awareness, not as exhaustive assurance standards.
- WSTG, MASTG, AI Testing Guide, and other testing guides provide procedures where applicable.
- Source version, status, URL, verification date, and freshness are recorded in the source registry.
- Complementary CIS, provider, NIST, or protocol guidance is clearly labeled when OWASP does not provide sufficient verification detail.
- Developing proposals are advisory and are never represented as released standards.

The authoritative project-level registry is [`source-registry.md`](../skill/review-software-security/references/source-registry.md). It links to source materials rather than redistributing their full text.
